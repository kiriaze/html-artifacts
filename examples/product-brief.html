Acme Sync

Sync your project files across machines without thinking about it.

Zero-config, git-aware file sync. Works offline and reconciles automatically on reconnect. Drop it in any project directory and walk away — your files follow you.

CLI-first Git-aware Offline-ready S3-compatible

Additional Context

What "git-aware" means in practice
Acme Sync reads .gitignore and respects it by default. Patterns in .syncignore extend that. Binary files in node_modules/, dist/, and build artifacts are never synced unless explicitly opted in.
Offline reconciliation approach
Changes made while offline are recorded in the local SQLite conflict log with a vector clock timestamp. On reconnect, the Diff Engine replays them against the remote state. Three-way merge for text; last-write-wins for binary. Conflicts that cannot be auto-resolved surface as named conflict copies (file.conflict.local / file.conflict.remote).

Risks

Risk Likelihood Impact Mitigation
Conflict resolution UX complexity
Users may not understand conflict copies or dismiss them, leading to data loss perception.
High High Ship interactive acmesync resolve wizard in v1.0; document with video walkthrough.
S3 rate limits under heavy fleets
Jordan's 40-VM use case could burst request rates during simultaneous VM starts.
Low Medium Exponential backoff + jitter in Sync Queue; configurable --max-concurrency flag.
SQLite corruption on hard shutdown
Abrupt power loss mid-write could corrupt the local conflict log.
Low High Enable WAL mode + PRAGMA synchronous=NORMAL; auto-repair on startup with integrity_check.
